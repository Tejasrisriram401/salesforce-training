# Day 1 - CRM Basics

## 1. What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

CRM is a technology used by companies to manage relationships with customers and prospects. It helps businesses store customer information, track interactions, manage sales, and improve customer satisfaction.

Examples of CRM activities:
- Tracking customer details
- Managing sales opportunities
- Following up with customers
- Recording emails and calls

---

## 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it is a cloud-based CRM platform that helps businesses manage their customers and sales processes efficiently.

Benefits of Salesforce:
- Stores customer data securely
- Improves communication between teams
- Tracks sales and opportunities
- Automates business processes
- Provides reports and dashboards
- Accessible from anywhere using the internet

---

## 3. Salesforce Objects

### Account
An Account represents a company or organization that a business works with.

Example:
- Infosys
- Amazon
- Mondocorp

---

### Contact
A Contact represents a person associated with an Account.

Example:
- Shawn Corbin working at Mondocorp

---

### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal with a customer.

Example:
- Replacement gas generator deal for Mondocorp

---

## 4. Real-World Mapping

| Real World | Salesforce Object |
|------------|------------------|
| Company | Account |
| Employee/Customer | Contact |
| Sales Deal | Opportunity |

Example:
- Mondocorp → Account
- Shawn Corbin → Contact
- Replacement gas generator → Opportunity
